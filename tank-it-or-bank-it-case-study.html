01 — Problem

AI is speeding up design… but slowing down thinking.

Teams were rapidly adopting AI tools like Figma AI and Google Stitch to generate screens — but without deeply understanding users, stakeholders, or the actual value being created.

What was happening
✕
Reaching for AI tools before defining the problem
✕
Surface-level solutions with weak problem framing
✕
No clear value proposition — just generated screens
✕
Cross-functional misalignment between designers, devs, and PMs
What needed to happen
✓
Deep user empathy before any tool is opened
✓
Structured problem framing with real constraints
✓
Value-driven design thinking as the foundation
✓
AI as an intentional enabler, not a crutch

03 — Process

Four phases of structured thinking

The workshop was crafted to force structured thinking before any AI tool was touched. Each phase built on the last, creating a layered learning arc.

01
Frame the Challenge
"Before you design, understand."
Teams received accessibility-driven problem cards — real human constraints like a mute user joining a Zoom call. This forced empathy and constraint-based thinking before any solution was considered.
Accessibility Empathy Problem Cards Constraint Thinking
02
Think Before AI
"Map the value before you build it."
Teams applied a simplified Value Proposition Framework — identifying user Jobs, Pains, and Gains, then mapping Product Pain Relievers and Gain Creators. The core differentiator of the workshop.
User side
Jobs to be done
What the user is trying to accomplish
Pains
Frustrations, risks, obstacles they face
Gains
Benefits and outcomes they desire
⟷
Product side
Pain relievers
How the solution reduces friction
Gain creators
How it creates value and delight
Value Prop Canvas Strategic Thinking Framework
03
AI as Enabler
"AI without thinking is just decoration."
Only after defining the problem, users, and value did teams open Google Stitch and Figma AI. The prompt became intentional — grounded in real insight, not guesswork. Outputs were iterated against the value framework.
Google Stitch Figma AI Intentional Prompting Iteration
04
The Pitch
"Not every fish is a shark — prove yours is."
Teams pitched to "Sharks" acting as VC judges. They had to justify their solution's value, defend their design decisions, and demonstrate real problem-solving. Judges "invested" — highest funding won. Thinking made tangible.
Shark Tank Format VC Pitch Design Defence Value Justification
04 — Challenges

What made this hard

Running a cross-functional workshop at scale surfaces real tensions. Here's what I navigated — and how.

CHALLENGE 01
Diverse Audience
Designers, developers, PMs, and architects all brought different mental models and levels of UX fluency.
Simplified frameworks with real-world examples made concepts accessible without losing depth for experienced participants.
CHALLENGE 02
AI Misconception
Most participants believed AI was an instant solution — something to reach for before any thinking happens.
Structured the workshop so AI was inaccessible until Phases 1 and 2 were complete. Forced the sequence, not just suggested it.
CHALLENGE 03
Personal Growth
Leading 50+ people live is high pressure. I started nervous, overthinking every transition and handoff.
By the second half I was engaging naturally, reading the room, and facilitating with confidence. A real inflection point.
